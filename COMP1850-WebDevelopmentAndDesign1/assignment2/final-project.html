Final Project

For my Final Project I will talk about one game that I usually play during my free time, Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO). The game expands upon the team-based action gameplay that the franchise pioneered when it was launched 12 years ago.

In 1999 Counter-Strike was released as a mod for Half-Life. As Counter-Strike progressed from a beta to a full release, it refined the classic gameplay that has come to define competitive shooters and produced a dedicated community that has followed the game for over a decade.

Through each of its iterations – 1.6, Source, and Global Offensive – Counter-Strike has been the de facto benchmark of a player’s skill. Teams from around the world are constantly demonstrating their ability and strategies in local, regional, and international tournaments.

During the past few years eSports became more and more recognized arround the world and today we have a lot of people living with money made from the game.

Also the competitive/professional scenario has grown in the past years, for example the last The International of Dota2 game had the total prize value of $ 10,000,000 USD. But the most interesting about this prize is that one item in-game was being sold to help with the prize, so the actual players of Dota2 arround the world came together to get this 10 milions dollars for the tournament.

CS:GO is getting bigger every year with more tournaments coming up, most of them happen in USA and in Europe, since most of the teams are stabilished in that places.

In this page I will talk about the CS:GO and for my final project I will talk about the competitive side of Counter-Strike Global Offensive, teams, tournaments, strategies and also how CS:GO is getting more popular.

Counter-Strike Global Offensive

Game modes

Arms Race: Arms race is a gun-progression mode featuring instant respawning and a ton of close-quarter combat. Players gain new weapons immediately after registering a kill as they work their way through each weapon in the game. Get a kill with the final weapon, a golden knife, and win the match.

Demolition: In demolition mode, players take turns attacking and defending a single bombsite in a series of maps designed for fast-paced gameplay. Players are automatically granted a starting weapon, and advance through a sequence of firearms when they register a kill. If you’re up to the challenge, you can try to register a kill each round and reach the ultimate weapons, powerful sniper rifles!

Casual: Casual mode, players automatically receive body armor and defusal kits and gain bonus rewards for kills.

Competitive: This is the classic gameplay that made Counter-Strike famous. Queue up and join a 5v5 best-of-30 match using standard competitive Counter-Strike rules on one of the classic maps. You can join a match on your own, or form a team and enter a match as a unit.
